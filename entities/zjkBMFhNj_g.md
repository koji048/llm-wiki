# Intro to Large Language Models

This general audience talk by Andrej Karpathy introduces large language models at an intuitive level. He explains that LLMs are neural networks trained on internet text to predict the next token, which compresses world knowledge into weights in a lossy probabilistic manner. Karpathy walks through the two-stage training: pre-training (compressing the internet) and alignment/post-training (making the model an assistant). He explains the context window, token sequences, and discusses emergent behaviors that arise at scale. The talk covers why LLMs are important, their capabilities, limitations, and the implications for AI development.

## Key Concepts

- Emergent Behaviors
